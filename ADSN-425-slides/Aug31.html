Timing & Spacing

Timing is the number of frames (drawings) that it takes for an action to occur.

Spacing is the amount of difference (space) between consecutive frames.

Slow In & Slow Out

Animations with slow ins and slow outs have their drawings bunched up at the beginnings and ends of actions.

This helps get rid of the mechanical feeling.

Anticipation

A small action before the big action. Usually in the opposite direction.

This helps the viewer have expectaions for the motion.

Follow Through

Similar to anticipation but at the end of an action.

This helps the motion feel grounded.

Overlapping Action

Different parts of an action are offset in time. Goes along with follow through and helps animations feel physically grounded.

"Loose parts" tend to lag behind the main action.

Squash & Stretch

Adds weight and flexibility to actions.

Stretchs looks best when you apply it in the same direction as the action. The opposite is true for squash.

Secondary Action

Details that are outside of the main action, but help to add emphasis or context.

Exaggeration

Animation and motion often must be very exaggerated. The goal is for the animation to just FEEL right.
