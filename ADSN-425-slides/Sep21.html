Meshes

What is a polygon mesh?

  1. A type of data that can describe any 3D object
  2. Made of vertices (points), edges (lines), & faces (flat shapes)
  3. Easy to edit and modify using most 3D software
  4. Each vertex can have data attached to it
  5. Each vertex and face have a direction – called a normal
  6. Can only ever have straight lines – therefore have a visual "resolution"

A cube has 8 verts

Edges

Faces

Normals

The normal is the opposite, or perpendicular, angle to a face. This tells the computer which way something is pointing, and determines how the lighting will look on that object.

Cameras

What is a camera?

  1. A transform (pos, rot, scale) from which we are viewing a 3D scene
  2. Need a camera to render – only way to tell the computer "where" to take the picture from
  3. Have tons of settings to describe how you want the computer to take the picture

Lights

What are Lights?

  1. Simulations of different real-world light sources
  2. Lights + meshes will define what scenes look like
  3. 4 major kinds – point, sun (directional), spot, & area

Materials

What are materials?

  1. Instructions that tell the computer how meshes should react to light sources
  2. Recently it has become possible to convincingly simululate many kinds of real world materials
  3. For now, principled BSDF – based on Diney + Pixar research and gives lots of properties
  4. Shader Editor – exposes a ton of control to create scenes ranging from realistic to stylized
